PREVENTATIVE MAINTENANCE TIPS TO KEEP YOUR PLUMBING IN TIP-TOP SHAPE


It’s always a good idea to perform preventative maintenance on your plumbing system to avoid costly and inconvenient repairs. Here are a few tips to help you keep your plumbing in top condition.

Inspect your plumbing regularly for wear and tear, such as leaks, cracks, or blockages.

Be on the lookout for any signs of wear and tear on your plumbing system, as they can lead to big problems down the road if not addressed. Homeowners should watch out for typical examples of plumbing issues, including leaks, cracks, and blockages.

Leaks are probably the most commonly encountered issue, and they can often be easy to fix if you catch them early on. All it takes is a quick call to a plumber to get things patched up before too much damage is done. Cracks in your pipes are also something you’ll want to address as soon as possible, as they can quickly turn into more significant problems down the road. On the other hand, blockages can cause serious drainage issues and even flooding if left unchecked.

It’s always a good idea to inspect your plumbing system regularly for any signs of wear and tear so that you can nip these problems in the bud before they have a chance to cause too much damage.

Use strainers in sink drains and showerheads to catch hair and other debris that can clog your pipes.

Clogged drains are a common problem in households all over the world. Hair, food particles, and other debris can accumulate in the drain and cause it to slow or stop up completely. This can be a significant inconvenience, as it can prevent water from flowing freely through the drain and lead to flooding. In some cases, clogged drains can even cause structural damage to your home.

There are several ways to deal with clogged drains. One popular method is to use a plunger to try to clear the obstruction. If this doesn’t work, you may have to call a plumber to remove it. Another option is to use a chemical drain cleaner. However, these cleaners can be dangerous if not used properly and damage your pipes.

Using filters in your sink drains and showerheads is safer and more environmentally friendly. These filters will catch hair and other debris before it has a chance to accumulate in the drain pipe and cause a clog. They are easy to install and affordable, making them an excellent option for anyone who wants to avoid dealing with clogged drains.

Flush your water heater every few months to remove sediment build-up.

Most people know they should flush their water heater every few months to remove sediment build-up, but many don’t understand why this is important.

Sediment build-up can cause several problems for your water heater, including decreased efficiency and shortened lifespan. By flushing your water heater regularly, you can avoid these problems and keep your water heater running smoothly for years to come.

There are a few simple steps you can follow to flush your water heater:

  1. Turn off the power to your water heater. If your water heater is gas-powered, turn off the gas supply.
  2. Drain the water from the tank by attaching a hose to the drain valve and opening it.
  3. Once the water has finished draining, close the drain valve and turn on the cold water supply. Open the pressure relief valve to allow air into the tank.
  4. When the tank is full, close the cold water supply and open all of the faucets in your home to release any air that may trap them.
  5. Turn the power to your water heater and check for leaks.

Don't pour grease or oil down the drain - dispose of it in a container instead.

Did you know that pouring grease or oil down the drain can clog your pipes and create a mess? Not only that, but it can also be harmful to the environment.

Pouring grease or oil down the drain is a bad habit many engage in without even realizing it. Grease and oil are difficult to break down, accumulating in gutters over time. This can lead to clogs, which will cause water to back up and spill out onto the floor. It can also cause damage to your plumbing system.

In addition, pouring grease or oil down the drain contributes to environmental pollution. When these substances end up in our waterways, they can contaminate and harm aquatic life. So it’s essential to dispose of grease and oil properly instead of just dumping them down the drain.

By following these simple tips, you can help ensure that your plumbing system stays in good working order for years to come.
